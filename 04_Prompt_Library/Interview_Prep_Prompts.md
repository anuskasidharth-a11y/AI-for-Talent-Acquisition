# Interview Prep Prompts

Prompts for preparing candidates, and for preparing yourself for interviewer-facing rounds.

## Candidate interview prep brief

```
The candidate has an interview for [role] at [company] with [interviewer
name/title if known]. Give them a short prep brief: 3 likely question
themes based on the JD, one story they should have ready (using their
background: [paste background]), and one smart question to ask the
interviewer.
```

## Behavioral answer structure (STAR)

```
Help me structure an answer to this likely interview question:
[paste question]
My relevant experience: [paste raw experience or bullet points]

Structure it as Situation, Task, Action, Result, in under 90 seconds
of spoken length. Keep the language natural, not scripted.
```

## Recruiter-side interviewer research

```
I'm interviewing with [interviewer name] for a [role, e.g. sourcer/
recruiter] position. Based on their public professional background
[paste any public bio/LinkedIn summary], suggest 3 topics they are
likely to probe on, and one thoughtful question I could ask them
specifically.
```

## Metrics presentation prep

```
Help me turn this raw metric into a confident, interview-ready one-liner:
[paste raw number, e.g. "improved offer conversion from 60% to 82%"]

Keep it to one sentence, quantify the improvement, and imply the
method without over-explaining.
```

## Addressing a known gap from past feedback

```
In a previous interview, I received this feedback: [paste feedback,
e.g. "gaps in Boolean search knowledge"]. Help me prepare a concise
way to address this proactively if it comes up again, without sounding
defensive, and a short example that demonstrates the skill now.
```

## Mock interview Q&A

```
Run a mock interview for a [role] position. Ask me 5 questions one
at a time, a mix of behavioral and technical/functional. After each
answer, give brief feedback before moving to the next question.
```
